  • Hurricanes and How to Prepare for Them

  • Power of a Hurricane

    Don't underestimate the power of a hurricane. A hurricane is a natural disaster that can bring devastation anywhere it goes.

    Here's how strong each category of a hurricane can get:

    Category 1-2

    Winds from 74 mph up to 110 mph

    Not a major hurricane, but can winds can still cause significant damge.

    Category 3

    Winds from 111 mph up to 129 mph

    Major Hurricane: Extremely dangerous winds that will cause major damage to homes and will up-root trees.

    Category 4

    Winds from 130 mph up to 156 mph

    Major Hurricane: Will produce catastrophic damage to structures. Power will be down for weeks or months.

    Category 5

    Winds from 156 mph and up

    Major Hurricane: Will produce catastrophic damage to structures to the point where places can become uninhabitable. Power will be down for months, and neighborhoods may become isolated due to damage.

  • History of Major Hurricanes in the United States

    Hurricane Andrew

    Category 5

    One of the most devastating hurricanes to hit the United States. Andrew was responsible for 23 deaths and $26.5 billion in damage in the United States alone.

    August 1992

    Hurricane Katrina

    Category 5

    The costliest and one of the deadliest hurricanes to hit the United States, causing $75 billion in damage in New Orleans and the Mississippi Coast. Katrina was also responsible for a reported 1200 deaths.

    August 2005

    Hurricane Irma

    Category 5

    One of the largest hurricanes to hit the U.S. since Katrina. According to reports, at least 73 people were killed in the Caribbean and the United States.

    August-September 2017

    Hurricane Maria

    Category 5

    Hurricane Maria caused a major humanitarian crisis in U.S. Territory Puerto Rico. As of October 2017, 89% of the island is left without power, the death toll has risen to 45 and at least 113 people remain missing.

    September 2017

  • Preparing for a Hurricane

    Being prepared can assure your survival during a major hurricane. Here's what you will need to do:

    1. Monitor local news for hurricane updates

    2. Stock up on water: Three gallons of water per person

    3. Stock up on food: buy non-perishable food items that will last at least three days

    4. Prepare a first aid kit How to build one

    5. Keep a flashlight and extra batteries

    6. Have a cellphone, and a portable charger You can find them on Amazon
